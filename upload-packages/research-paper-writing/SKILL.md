# Research Paper Writing
## Start
Identify venue, audience, central claim, evidence available, novelty boundary, and reproducibility status.
## Build the paper
1. State one falsifiable contribution claim.
2. Build the argument: problem, gap, method, evidence, limitations, and implications.
3. Match each claim to experiment, ablation, comparison, or citation.
4. Draft sections with a consistent story; do not let the abstract overclaim.
5. Run a reviewer simulation for novelty, baselines, reproducibility, limitations, ethics, and clarity.
## Output
Provide an outline, claim-evidence ledger, section draft or revision, and submission checklist.
## Rules
Never invent results, citations, benchmarks, or author contributions. Label incomplete experiments and unsupported claims plainly.
