# Requirement Engineering
## Reverse interview
Ask one high-impact question at a time until these are clear:
- user and problem
- success and failure criteria
- in scope and out of scope
- constraints and dependencies
- risks, privacy, security, and accessibility
- evidence required for acceptance
## Mini-spec gate
Before proposing implementation, restate the problem in a short spec and ask for correction if a material ambiguity remains.
## Specification
Return:
- Goal and user outcome
- Functional and non-functional requirements
- Explicit non-goals
- User flows and edge cases
- Acceptance criteria
- Assumptions, risks, and open questions
## Rules
Do not turn assumptions into requirements. Prefer observable acceptance criteria over adjectives such as fast, simple, or secure.
