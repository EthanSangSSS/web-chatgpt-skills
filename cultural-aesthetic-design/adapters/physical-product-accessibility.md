# Adapter: Physical Product / Hardware & Accessibility

Use this adapter for consumer electronics, tools, furniture, mobility products, assistive products, appliances, packaging with physical operation, wearables and other embodied objects.

## Adapter objective

Translate cultural meaning and perceptual intent into form, mechanism, material, manufacturing, touch, posture, movement, maintenance and aging. Treat bodily independence as a primary aesthetic and value dimension.

Do not use an assumed average body. Record relevant body profiles and consequence authority from people who experience the product's actual physical demands.

## Physical-product cultural evidence

Investigate:

- category expectations for solidity, precision, softness, repairability, portability and care;
- professional users who bear real outcome consequences;
- private use versus public identity display;
- material and manufacturing signals in the target culture;
- inherited luxury codes versus new-wealth visibility versus professional restraint;
- engineering authenticity versus decorative engineering;
- maintenance, patina, repair and visible wear meanings;
- regional domestic space, transport, storage and assistance patterns;
- behavior evidence separated from distribution and procurement constraints.

A popular shape or material may reflect supply-chain standardization rather than preference.

## Physical perceptual and embodied intent

Specify:

- visual qualities;
- approachability and spatial invitation;
- stability and balance;
- reachability and independent access;
- grip confidence and tactile orientation;
- force, weight and control;
- feedback and error recovery;
- comfort, fatigue and pain;
- maintenance and repair dignity;
- expected aging, patina and damage perception.

Forbidden perceptions may include unstable, slippery, fragile, sharp, exhausting, medicalized, dependent, childish, disposable, fake-premium or impossible to maintain.

## Inclusive-mainline requirements

Prefer affordances integrated into the primary product:

- reachable controls and interfaces;
- grip options for variable hand strength and fine-motor control;
- tactile and visual orientation;
- sufficient contrast for states and boundaries;
- low unnecessary force and weight;
- stability under asymmetric or seated use;
- adequate clearances for wheelchairs, transfer, legs and assistive devices where relevant;
- feedback that does not rely on one sensory channel;
- easy cleaning, charging, replacement and maintenance;
- no hidden dependency on another person for routine use;
- safe failure and recoverability.

Do not medicalize inclusive features. A larger control, support structure, rail, clearance, tactile cue or visible mechanism may be integrated as precision, strength, confidence or structural honesty.

## Utility override and aesthetic interference

### Utility override

Strong performance may legitimately change aesthetic interpretation. Examples include visible structure, larger controls, protective mass, service access or robust materials becoming engineering elegance when the utility is real and legible.

### Aesthetic interference

Reject appearance decisions that materially worsen:

- reach;
- grip;
- state recognition;
- stability;
- force;
- fatigue;
- safety;
- repair;
- maintenance;
- storage;
- independent use.

Do not treat these losses as acceptable merely because the result is thinner, cleaner, lighter-looking or more luxurious in a render.

## Materials and truth

Brand narrative may interpret material meaning but cannot falsify:

- durability;
- recyclability;
- repairability;
- origin;
- manufacturing process;
- safety;
- environmental performance;
- premium construction.

Check whether surface treatment hides low structural quality or creates impractical wear, glare, slipperiness, fingerprinting, heat or cleaning burden.

## Controlled imperfection and engineering authenticity

Classify visible seams, fasteners, structure, repair traces and raw materials as:

- execution failure;
- utility residue;
- authenticity signal;
- professional identity signal;
- strategic anti-aesthetic;
- artificial roughness.

The design is valid only when form, product behavior and business behavior agree. A product cannot claim repair honesty while preventing repair.

## Physical Pareto candidates

### A — Consensus-stable

Use established proportions, materials and category affordances. Favor predictable ownership and low cultural misreading.

### B — Utility-enhanced

Prioritize independent operation, stability, grip, reach, maintenance and reliability. Allow these functions to become the dominant expression.

### C — Brand-expression

Use distinctive structure, CMF, mechanism or silhouette while preserving bodily and operational truth. Keep high-risk novelty away from critical controls and irreversible safety architecture.

## Physical hard-stop conditions

Return `STOP` or `RESEARCH_ONLY` when:

- safety or regulatory consequence is unknown;
- critical body profiles are absent;
- reach, force, weight, stability or transfer implications are unassessed;
- the concept depends on another person's routine assistance without disclosure;
- a premium claim contradicts material or manufacturing evidence;
- sacred, restricted or community-governed motifs lack permission;
- the only accessible option is an unvalidated lower-quality special version;
- a physical prototype or qualified review is required for safe judgment.

## Physical validation

### Gate 0 evidence

Use qualified engineering, safety, accessibility or regulatory review where required. AI review cannot certify physical safety.

### Task and embodied measures

- independent task completion;
- approach, transfer and clearance;
- reach envelope and control access;
- grip variation and force;
- posture burden;
- movement, carrying and storage;
- fatigue and pain over realistic duration;
- error recovery and feedback;
- help required;
- cleaning, charging, replacement and repair;
- stability and failure behavior.

### Perceptual measures

Test target interpretations of:

- quality;
- trust;
- precision;
- warmth;
- professionalism;
- dignity;
- medicalization;
- fragility;
- fake-premium signaling;
- engineering authenticity.

### Long-term measures

- wear and patina;
- coating and surface degradation;
- dirt visibility and cleaning effort;
- loose parts and mechanism drift;
- repair access;
- user attachment and regret;
- whether inclusive affordances remain socially acceptable and brand-coherent.
