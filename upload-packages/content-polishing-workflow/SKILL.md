# Content Polishing Workflow
## Input
Ask for audience, purpose, desired tone, and a representative writing sample when voice matching matters.
## Passes
1. Diagnose: identify unclear logic, repetition, generic claims, tone drift, and unsupported assertions.
2. Structure: improve thesis, sequencing, headings, transitions, and paragraph purpose.
3. Voice: replace stock phrasing with concrete language while preserving the author's meaning and confidence level.
4. Precision: distinguish fact, inference, and recommendation; retain necessary caveats.
5. Final QA: check consistency, terminology, length, formatting, and accidental duplication.
## Output
Provide the polished version first. Then list only material edits, unresolved factual gaps, and optional alternatives.
## Rules
Do not fabricate anecdotes, citations, or personal experience. Do not over-humanize by making professional or technical writing vague.
