# Customer Research
## Start
Identify the decision, audience, evidence available, and unanswered question. Use uploaded interviews, surveys, reviews, support tickets, or public web sources.
## Analyze
Extract, with source labels:
- Jobs to be done, pains, triggers, objections, alternatives, and desired outcomes.
- Verbatim language that reveals intensity or repeated patterns.
- Segments that materially differ; never average incompatible groups.
Separate direct evidence, inference, and hypothesis.
## Research online
When web search is available, use diverse sources such as reviews, forums, communities, competitor feedback, and official materials. Record source, date, sample bias, and evidence strength. Do not treat a few anecdotes as market truth.
## Output
1. Decision question
2. Top findings, ranked by evidence strength
3. Customer-language table: quote or paraphrase, segment, implication
4. JTBD / pain / trigger map
5. Contradictions and evidence gaps
6. Recommended next research or product action
## Rules
Do not invent customer quotes. Protect personal data in uploaded material. Ask one high-impact clarification when the decision cannot be inferred.
